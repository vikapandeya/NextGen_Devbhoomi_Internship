Certifications — Roadmap
CompTIA

Security+

Industry baseline certification. Required by many employers as a minimum. The most recognized entry-level security cert globally. Covers threats, cryptography, network security, identity management, and more.

Exam: ~₹25,000  ·  Entry Level
EC-Council

CEH — Certified Ethical Hacker

Well-known specifically in India. Good for penetration testing roles and government/defense sector. Multiple choice exam — not hands-on. Widely recognized by Indian IT services companies.

Exam: ~₹45,000 + training  ·  Intermediate
Offensive Security

OSCP — Offensive Security Certified Professional

24-hour hands-on exam — you must compromise machines under time pressure. The gold standard for penetration testers globally. Highly respected; carries genuine weight in job applications.

Lab + Exam: ~₹60,000  ·  Advanced
(ISC)²

CISSP — Certified Information Systems Security Professional

For experienced security professionals with 5+ years. Covers management and technical domains. Highly regarded for security manager and CISO roles. Requires proven experience to sit the exam.

Exam: ~₹45,000  ·  Senior Level (5+ yrs)
Amazon Web Services

AWS Security Specialty

Demonstrates cloud security expertise on AWS. High demand as companies move workloads to cloud. Covers IAM, data protection, infrastructure security, incident response on AWS.

Exam: ~₹22,000  ·  Intermediate (AWS experience needed)
Various Providers

DFIR — Digital Forensics & Incident Response

Growing specialized field. EC-Council CHFI (Certified Hacking Forensic Investigator) or SANS FOR508. Covers disk forensics, memory analysis, network forensics, and legal evidence handling.

Varies by provider  ·  Intermediate–Advanced

90-Day Action Plan — From Internship to First Job

Your 90-Day Launch Plan

1

Days 1–30: Certify & Build Your Presence

Security is a credentialed field — get your first certification early

  • Study for and attempt CompTIA Security+ — use Professor Messer's free study guide (professormesser.com)
  • Create a TryHackMe profile and complete at least 10 rooms including the Pre-Security path
  • Write 2 LinkedIn posts about what you learned (security content performs well — post about a CVE you studied or a lab you completed)
  • Publish your DVWA pen test report writeup on GitHub — clearly label it "Practice environment only"
2

Days 31–60: Apply, Practice CTFs, Build a Home Lab

Parallel track: applying while actively strengthening your skills

  • Apply to SOC Analyst and Junior Security Analyst roles — LinkedIn, Naukri, and direct company career pages
  • Participate in a beginner CTF competition — PicoCTF (free) or search CTFTime.org for upcoming events
  • Complete the free Splunk Fundamentals 1 course at education.splunk.com (SIEM is the #1 tool in SOC roles)
  • Build a home lab: Kali Linux VM + Windows target VM + DVWA container — practice in isolation
3

Days 61–90: Interview Preparation & Convert

Security interviews test practical knowledge — prepare accordingly

  • Security interviews: explain a real CVE you've studied in detail (research CVE-2021-44228 Log4Shell or MS17-010 EternalBlue)
  • Practice drawing a network architecture diagram with security controls from memory (DMZ, firewall, IDS, bastion host)
  • Walk through a complete incident response scenario verbally — detection, containment, eradication, recovery, lessons learned
  • Target CERT-In empaneled companies for pen test roles, and BFSI sector companies (banks hire large numbers of security analysts due to RBI compliance requirements)
