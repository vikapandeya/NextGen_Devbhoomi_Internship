Career Roadmap — Data Science & Analytics

Job Roles & Salary Progression

The data science career ladder in the Indian market as of 2026. Salaries are CTC ranges; actual offers vary by company tier, city, and your portfolio strength.

🌱
Data Analyst
0–1 year experience
₹3.5 – 7 LPA
Core Skills:
  • Excel / Google Sheets (advanced: pivot tables, VLOOKUP, Power Query)
  • SQL — SELECT, GROUP BY, JOIN, subqueries
  • Power BI or Tableau for dashboards
  • Descriptive statistics — mean, median, distributions
  • Python basics (pandas, Matplotlib) as a bonus
Day-to-Day Work:

Building weekly/monthly reports, creating executive dashboards, answering ad-hoc data questions from business teams, maintaining data quality in spreadsheets and databases.

⚡
Junior Data Scientist
1–2 years experience
₹6 – 12 LPA
Core Skills:
  • Python (pandas, sklearn, Matplotlib, Seaborn) — fluent
  • ML basics: LogReg, Random Forest, cross-validation
  • Feature engineering and data preprocessing
  • A/B test analysis and hypothesis testing
  • SQL for data extraction from production databases
Day-to-Day Work:

Building predictive models under senior guidance, running A/B tests, creating feature pipelines, analyzing experiment results, writing Python scripts for data workflows.

🔧
Data Scientist
2–4 years experience
₹12 – 22 LPA
Core Skills:
  • Advanced ML: XGBoost, LightGBM, Neural networks
  • NLP or Computer Vision (specialization)
  • Experiment design and causal inference
  • Stakeholder communication — translating models to business value
  • Model deployment basics: REST APIs, Docker
Day-to-Day Work:

Owning end-to-end ML projects, defining problem formulation, presenting findings to product/business teams, mentoring junior analysts, improving model performance in production.

🚀
Senior Data Scientist
4–7 years experience
₹22 – 40 LPA
Core Skills:
  • Research and model architecture design
  • Production ML systems — reliability, latency, monitoring
  • Technical mentoring and code review
  • Cross-functional leadership with product and engineering
  • Strong business acumen — ROI, prioritization
Day-to-Day Work:

Designing ML strategy for products, mentoring junior/mid-level scientists, architecting model systems, writing technical documents, influencing product roadmap with data insights.

☁️
ML Engineer
2–5 years experience
₹15 – 35 LPA
Core Skills:
  • MLOps: MLflow, Kubeflow, SageMaker, Vertex AI
  • Model serving: FastAPI, TorchServe, TF Serving
  • Kubernetes + Docker for scalable deployments
  • Feature stores: Feast, Tecton
  • Model monitoring: data drift, concept drift, alerting
Day-to-Day Work:

Taking ML models from notebooks to production, building CI/CD pipelines for models, monitoring production model performance, building feature pipelines, ensuring model reliability at scale.

💼
Data Science Manager / Lead
6+ years experience
₹35 – 70+ LPA
Core Skills:
  • Team leadership — hiring, performance reviews, growth plans
  • Roadmap planning — prioritizing DS projects by business impact
  • Cross-functional alignment with product, engineering, and business
  • Stakeholder management — C-suite presentations
  • Technical depth to mentor and review team output
Day-to-Day Work:

Running sprint planning for DS team, hiring interviews, presenting to VP/C-suite, making build-vs-buy decisions for ML tools, setting standards for code quality and model documentation.

Recommended Certifications

Certifications signal commitment and structured learning to employers. Prioritize free or affordable options with strong industry recognition.

🎓
Google Data Analytics Certificate
Coursera (Google)
Free with audit · ₹2,000–3,000/month for certificate

8-course series covering the full data analyst workflow. Uses R, SQL, and Tableau. Widely recognized by recruiters, especially for analyst roles at MNCs. Great starting point — 6 months at 10 hrs/week.

Covers: SQL, R, Tableau, spreadsheets, data cleaning, visualization, case study capstone
🎓
IBM Data Science Professional Certificate
Coursera (IBM)
~₹3,000/month (approx 3–4 months to complete)

10-course series covering Python, Jupyter Notebook, data analysis, visualization, machine learning, and a capstone project. Strong on pandas, sklearn, and Folium. IBM badge adds credibility for IT services roles.

Covers: Python, pandas, Matplotlib, scikit-learn, ML fundamentals, SQL, Applied DS capstone
☁️
AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty
Amazon Web Services
~₹25,000 exam fee · Study materials vary

Advanced certification for production ML on AWS. Covers SageMaker (training, deployment, monitoring), data prep for ML, and MLOps on AWS. High value for cloud-focused roles. Requires 1–2 years ML experience to pass comfortably.

Covers: SageMaker, S3, Glue, Rekognition, feature engineering, ML security, cost optimization
☁️
Microsoft Azure Data Scientist Associate (DP-100)
Microsoft
~₹12,000 exam fee

Validates ability to use Azure Machine Learning to train, deploy, and monitor ML models. Strong choice if you're targeting Microsoft ecosystem companies or the large number of enterprises using Azure. More accessible than AWS ML Specialty.

Covers: Azure ML Studio, AutoML, MLflow, model deployment, responsible AI
🏆
Kaggle Certifications (Python, ML, Pandas)
Kaggle (Google)
Completely Free

Short, practical micro-courses with immediate exercises in Kaggle notebooks. Highly respected in the data science community because they're completion-based and practical. Complete Python + Pandas + Intro ML as a minimum. Kaggle also has a competition ranking that's very visible to employers.

Covers: Python, pandas, ML intro, feature engineering, deep learning, NLP, SQL, geospatial analysis
🧠
Deep Learning Specialization
Coursera (deeplearning.ai, Andrew Ng)
~₹3,000/month (approx 3 months)

The gold standard for learning deep learning. Andrew Ng's 5-course series covers neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, and practical advice for structuring ML projects. Essential if you want to work on computer vision, NLP, or generative AI.

Covers: Neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, LSTM, seq2seq, batch norm, hyperparameter tuning, TensorFlow

90-Day Post-Program Action Plan

What to do after completing the internship to maximize your job search chances.

Phase 1

Days 1–30: Polish & Publish

  • Export all 3 project notebooks to HTML and publish on GitHub Pages (free) or nbviewer.org
  • Create a Kaggle profile and make your capstone notebook public — add a proper dataset description
  • Polish your GitHub README files: project overview, how-to-run instructions, key results with numbers
  • Update your LinkedIn profile: add "Data Science Intern — NextGen Devbhoomi Foundation" with a 2-sentence description of your 3 projects
  • Write and publish 1 LinkedIn article: "5 Things I Learned Analyzing the Titanic Dataset" — drives engagement and shows communication skills
  • Get your Kaggle Python, Pandas, and Intro ML certificates (free, takes 1–2 weeks total)
Phase 2

Days 31–60: Apply & Practice

  • Solve 3 Kaggle beginner competitions — Titanic (classification), House Prices (regression), and one of your choice. Work toward a top-30% leaderboard score on at least one.
  • Apply to Data Analyst and Junior Data Scientist roles on Naukri.com and LinkedIn. Set up job alerts for "data analyst", "business analyst", "junior data scientist" in your target cities.
  • Join a DS study group — Kaggle Discord, Analytics Vidhya community, or a local data science meetup
  • Practice 3 SQL problems per day on StrataScratch.com or mode.com/sql-tutorial — focus on window functions and complex JOINs
  • Do mock interviews with a friend or use Pramp.com for free technical mock interviews
Phase 3

Days 61–90: Interview & Negotiate

  • Prepare the "explain your model" narrative for each project: problem → data → approach → result → business impact. Practice until you can do it in 3 minutes without looking at notes.
  • Research the data stack of every company you interview with. Know whether they use Spark, BigQuery, Redshift, dbt, or Tableau — mention it during interviews.
  • Prepare your SQL showcase: have 2 complex queries ready to discuss (ideally from a real problem you solved). Be ready to write them live in an interview.
  • Study statistics interview questions: mean vs median when to use each, explain p-value to a non-statistician, what is overfitting and how do you fix it.
  • Negotiate salary: research market rates on AmbitionBox and Glassdoor. Junior DS roles at product companies start at ₹8–12 LPA in metro cities. Know your floor before negotiating.

Top Employers — India Data Science Market

Analytics Firms

These companies specialize in data analytics as a service for large enterprises. Often hire freshers in bulk and provide excellent training.

  • Fractal Analytics — Top analytics firm, strong DS culture, works with Fortune 500
  • Tiger Analytics — ML-heavy, works with FMCG, banking, retail clients
  • Mu Sigma — Large-scale analytics, focuses on decision science, Bangalore HQ
  • LatentView Analytics — Listed company, strong in digital analytics, Chennai
  • TheMathCompany — Boutique analytics firm, hands-on ML projects

Product Companies

India's tech unicorns have large DS teams working on live products with millions of users. High impact, fast learning, and excellent compensation.

  • Swiggy — Demand forecasting, restaurant recommendations, surge pricing
  • Ola / Namma Yatri — Ride matching, pricing, driver incentives
  • Paytm / PhonePe — Fraud detection, credit scoring, financial insights
  • CRED — Credit analytics, behavioral data science
  • Flipkart / Meesho — Recommendation systems, search ranking, logistics

IT Services

Large IT firms have dedicated data and AI practice groups. Good for structured career paths and exposure to diverse client problems.

  • Accenture Analytics / Accenture AI — Large practice, diverse client industries
  • Capgemini Insights & Data — Strong European client base, good training programs
  • Wipro Holmes AI — AI platform, NLP and automation-heavy
  • TCS Data Intelligence — Large-scale data engineering and analytics

MNC India Research & Engineering

Global companies with significant India R&D centers. Highly competitive but offer world-class DS work, compensation, and international collaboration.

  • Amazon (Alexa AI, AWS, Retail) — Multiple DS teams across Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai
  • Microsoft Research India — Research-oriented, Bangalore, cutting-edge ML
  • Google DeepMind India — Research and product DS, Bangalore
  • Goldman Sachs Quant Engineering — Quantitative finance DS, Bangalore
